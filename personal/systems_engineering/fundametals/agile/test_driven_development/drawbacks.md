# Drawbacks

- Requires a lot of time and effort up front, making development feel slow initially.

- Focusing on the simplest design now without thinking ahead can lead to major refactoring.

- Difficult to write good tests that cover essentials and avoid superfluous tests.

- If design changes rapidly, tests must change; risk wasting time on tests for dropped features.

- Maintaining the test suite takes time and effort and must be reconfigured for maximum value.
